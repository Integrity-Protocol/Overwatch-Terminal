## Core Thesis
Project Atlas has developed a novel sovereign debt architecture in which a Fixed-Rate Note (AFN) maintains par value ($100.00) through an automated debt-to-equity swap mechanism with a free-floating equity instrument (Atlas Sovereign Equity, ASE). The thesis holds that this mechanism can maintain the AFN's par value under stress conditions, that the High-Yield Treasury Facility will sustain institutional deposits through competitive yields, and that the Stabilization Consortium's collateral reserves provide adequate backstop against systemic redemption pressure.

## Position
Monitoring structural health of the Atlas Fixed-Rate Note par stability, the debt-to-equity swap mechanism, institutional deposit concentration, and reserve adequacy. This is an analytical framework evaluating whether the architecture is fundamentally sound or structurally fragile.

## Compound Stress Matrix
Three systemic indicators that compound through interconnected mechanisms:

MONITORING: Any one indicator in elevated range.
ELEVATED: Two of (AFN deviation > $0.50, Facility net outflows > $500M/period, ASE price decline > 15%/period) simultaneously.
CRITICAL: Two of (AFN deviation > $2.00, Facility net outflows > $2B/period, ASE price decline > 30%/period) OR Stabilization Consortium forced deployment.
EMERGENCY: Consortium collateral exhausted + swap mechanism producing > 50% ASE supply growth/period + AFN deviation > $5.00.

The break point of a pre-loaded structure is LOWER than the break point of an unloaded structure. If one leg is already elevated, less force is needed from the others to reach critical.
